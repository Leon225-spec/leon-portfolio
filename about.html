Who I Am

I came into this world on June 5, 2006, in a tiny village just outside Harare. My earliest memories are of the dusty roads of Zaka, Masvingo, where I grew up under the loving care of my mother and her sisters—my pillars of strength when life felt uncertain. I attended St. Anthony’s High School, dreaming of blueprints and buildings, but fate led me to Speciss College, where I discovered my true passion: Computing and Information Systems. With determination, I earned my diploma through ABMA, and since then I’ve poured my heart into every line of code.

I taught myself by building projects that reflected my hopes and challenges: a heartfelt romantic website crafted just for joy; **zimSMS**, a bold idea to empower small organizations with SMS alerts—an idea that stumbled without API funding; **Tronix Inventory Management System**, where I breathe life into the frontend alongside a friend; and finally, my personal portfolio, a minimalist shrine to my journey, my skills, and my stories.

When the keyboard rests, my mind wanders to entrepreneurship—I study new ideas as eagerly as I once sketched building designs. I often think: “If I hadn’t fallen in love with computers, I would have followed my dreams of architecture.” Yet, it was Mark Zuckerberg’s journey that ignited my own. My path hasn’t been easy. My father passed away when I was only in Grade 2, leaving a void I could barely understand. But in his absence, the love of my mother and her sisters became my guiding light. Friends and brothers have rallied around me, and their belief in me fuels my promise: one day, I will make them proud. Each challenge, each line of code, brings me closer to that promise—and to honoring the memory of my father with every success.
